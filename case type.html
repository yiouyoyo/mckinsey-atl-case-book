Section 01

The Top-Down Approach

McKinsey cases reward structured, hypothesis-first thinking. Lead with your conclusion, support with evidence, and never lose the thread. Here's the 8-step flow.

STEP 01
Restate & Clarify the Objective
Paraphrase the prompt back to the interviewer. Confirm you understood the goal, scope, and definition of success. Ask 1–2 targeted clarifying questions.
💡 "Just to make sure I understand — the client wants to... is that correct? And are we focused on the next 12 months?"
STEP 02
Structure Your Approach
Before diving in, lay out your framework. Tell the interviewer how you'll break down the problem. MECE thinking — mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive.
💡 "I'd like to look at this across three areas: revenue, cost, and market dynamics. Let me start with revenue."
STEP 03
Gather Company & Market Context
Ask about the company's size, position, business model, and competitive landscape. Connect the company's mission to the case objective.
💡 "Can you tell me more about where this company sits in the market? Who are the main competitors?"
STEP 04
Form a Hypothesis Early
Don't wait until the end. State an early directional hypothesis upfront — this shows McKinsey-style top-down thinking. Refine as you get data.
💡 "My early hypothesis is that the profitability issue is on the cost side, likely in COGS — I'd like to test that."
STEP 05
Drive the Analysis
Request data, run the math, probe each branch. Support or disprove your hypothesis with evidence. Show comfort with numbers — McKinsey tests quantitative fluency.
💡 Do the math out loud. Walk through your calculation step by step. State your assumptions explicitly.
STEP 06
Synthesize — the "So What"
Pull the threads together. What does the data actually tell you? This is your synthesis moment — move from observation to insight.
💡 "So what this tells us is... which means the real issue isn't X, it's Y."
STEP 07
Recommendation — Lead with the Answer
Confident, clear, top-down. State your conclusion first, then support it. Think of it like a McKinsey slide: the headline is the insight, not the data.
💡 "I recommend the client pursue X. There are three reasons: first... second... third..."
STEP 08
Risks, Caveats & Next Steps
Acknowledge what you'd want to validate, key risks, and immediate actions. Shows intellectual honesty and real-world thinking — not just "the answer."
💡 "The main risk here is X. I'd want to validate Y before committing. Immediate next step would be..."

Sections 02 – 06

Case Type Playbooks

Five core case archetypes. Each has a distinct opening hypothesis, key data asks, and common traps. Know the pattern, then flex.

Growth Strategy
📈

Client wants to grow revenue. Where, how fast, and through what lever — organic vs. inorganic, product vs. geography?

  • Current state: revenue breakdown by product / segment / geo
  • Market sizing: TAM, SAM, growth rate
  • Competitive dynamics: market share, differentiation
  • Levers: price, volume, new products, new markets
  • Go-to-market: distribution, channels, CAC vs. LTV
  • Prioritize: which lever has highest ROI + feasibility?
New Market Entry
🌐

Should we enter? If yes, how? Evaluate market attractiveness vs. company fit vs. competitive barriers.

  • Market attractiveness: size, growth, profitability
  • Competitive landscape: incumbents, barriers to entry
  • Company fit: capabilities, synergies, brand fit
  • Entry mode: organic, acquire, partner/JV
  • Risks & timeline to profitability
  • Go / no-go recommendation with clear rationale
Profitability
💰

Profits are declining. Isolate whether it's a revenue problem, a cost problem, or both — then drill down.

  • Revenue: price × volume — which moved? Why?
  • Cost: fixed vs. variable — what changed?
  • COGS vs. SG&A vs. other line items
  • Segment: which product / geography is the drag?
  • Benchmarking: vs. competitors, vs. prior periods
  • Root cause → recommendation to restore margins
Mergers & Acquisitions
🤝

Should we acquire? What's the strategic rationale, and does the value creation justify the price?

  • Strategic fit: why this target, why now?
  • Target analysis: financials, market position, capabilities
  • Synergies: revenue synergies + cost synergies
  • Valuation: is the price reasonable given synergies?
  • Integration risk: culture, ops, retention
  • Alternative: build vs. buy vs. partner?
Cost Reduction
✂️

Client needs to reduce costs without hurting core business. Find where, how much, and what the tradeoffs are.

  • Cost structure: map all cost categories
  • Benchmark: vs. industry / competitors
  • Quick wins vs. structural changes
  • Procurement, headcount, footprint, outsourcing
  • Impact on revenue / quality / morale
  • Prioritized roadmap with savings estimates

Section 07

Industry-Specific Guides

Each industry has its own vocabulary, KPIs, and business model logic. Know the lens before you walk in.

Key Concepts & KPIs

  • Payers vs. providers vs. pharma — know who your client is
  • PMPM — per member per month cost
  • Utilization rate — volume of services consumed
  • Reimbursement rates — Medicare/Medicaid vs. commercial
  • HEDIS scores — quality measurement for managed care
  • Patient outcomes, readmission rates, LOS
  • Value-based care vs. fee-for-service

Common Case Angles

  • Hospital system profitability under value-based contracts
  • Pharma company entering biosimilar market
  • Health insurer reducing medical loss ratio
  • Digital health startup scaling patient acquisition
  • AI/ML integration in clinical workflows (very relevant now)
  • Cost of care management for high-risk populations

Key Concepts & KPIs

  • Same-store sales growth (SSS) — organic performance
  • Gross margin % — varies widely by category
  • Inventory turns — efficiency of stock management
  • Shrink — loss from theft, damage, error
  • Basket size & traffic — volume drivers
  • Omnichannel mix — online vs. in-store economics

Common Case Angles

  • Big-box retailer (e.g. Home Depot) reducing supply chain costs
  • DTC brand expanding into physical retail
  • Grocery chain responding to margin compression
  • Retailer digital transformation / e-commerce acceleration
  • Private label strategy to improve margins
  • Store network optimization / closure analysis

Key Concepts & KPIs

  • Brand equity & NPS
  • Market share by channel
  • Price elasticity — sensitivity of demand to price
  • Trade promotion ROI
  • CAC vs. LTV — especially for D2C
  • SKU rationalization — simplifying portfolio

Common Case Angles

  • CPG brand losing market share to private label
  • Premium brand entering mass market
  • Consumer company pricing strategy in inflationary environment
  • Portfolio rationalization — which SKUs to cut?
  • International expansion of US consumer brand
  • Direct-to-consumer channel build vs. buy

Key Concepts & KPIs

  • COGS breakdown — raw materials, labor, overhead
  • Inventory days / DSI
  • On-time in-full (OTIF) — delivery performance
  • Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3 suppliers
  • Make vs. buy vs. outsource
  • Design for supply chain (DFSC) — cost set at design
  • Lead time, safety stock, EOQ

Common Case Angles

  • Manufacturer reducing COGS through design optimization
  • Retailer building supply chain resilience post-disruption
  • Company nearshoring vs. offshoring decision
  • Flooring / home goods supplier managing volatility
  • Procurement consolidation for cost savings
  • Digital supply chain transformation / real-time visibility

Section 08

AI @ McKinsey

AI is now a live topic in McKinsey cases and interviews. Know what the firm is building, where it's deploying, and how to bring AI fluency into your answers.

QuantumBlack
McKinsey's AI arm

McKinsey's AI practice is led by QuantumBlack, its data science and AI subsidiary. The firm has invested heavily in proprietary AI tools, GenAI client offerings, and internal productivity platforms. AI literacy is now expected at the BA/analyst level — especially for Tech & AI track candidates.

What McKinsey Is Building

  • Lilli — McKinsey's internal GenAI knowledge platform for consultants
  • QuantumBlack AI — analytics, ML, decision intelligence for clients
  • GenAI client transformation practices across industries
  • AI-powered strategy tools embedded in consulting workflows
  • Responsible AI frameworks and governance advisory

AI Case Topics Being Tested

  • Should a company build, buy, or partner for AI capabilities?
  • ROI of GenAI deployment — where does value actually accrue?
  • AI-driven cost reduction (automation, workforce reallocation)
  • Data strategy as a prerequisite to AI value creation
  • AI in healthcare — clinical AI, coding automation, diagnostics
  • Responsible AI / risk management for regulated industries

How to Talk About AI in Cases

  • Don't just say "use AI" — specify the use case and value chain step
  • Quantify: "AI could reduce X hours of manual work by Y%"
  • Always address data readiness: "This assumes clean, labeled data exists"
  • Acknowledge change management: "Adoption risk is real — need training"
  • Connect to your own experience where genuine (MedEase, ANNA-PR)
